What Is a Fractional CHRO?

A Fractional CHRO is an experienced HR strategist who supports organizations on a part-time basis, with long-term commitment and deep integration. In contrast to traditional interim management, which often focuses on temporary vacancy coverage, the Fractional CHRO acts as a stable member of the leadership team.

This role carries a central leadership mandate: the Fractional CHRO relieves the CEO in all strategic people matters, complements the executive team with a Human Capital perspective, and consistently represents it at C-level.

The boutique advantage: While large providers often sell standardized processes, a specialized approach such as TaVyro.ch offers focused precision and direct senior expertise without agency overhead.

Why Strategic HR Leadership Is Often Missing in SMEs

Many SMEs lack access to strategic HR leadership at executive level, not because the need is absent, but because the structure does not support it.

  • No CHRO at executive level: People topics remain fragmented across functions.
  • A full-time CHRO is often not economically viable: the need exists, but not at a permanent 100% executive cost base.
  • HR teams are operationally overloaded: there is limited capacity for strategic leadership, governance, and transformation.

The result is not neutral. It creates hidden follow-on costs in leadership quality, organizational effectiveness, employee retention, and culture.

The Fractional CHRO model addresses exactly this structural gap by providing targeted executive-level HR leadership without creating a permanent fixed cost structure.

A full-time CHRO remains the right model for larger or highly complex organizations. For many SMEs, however, it is structurally disproportionate.
