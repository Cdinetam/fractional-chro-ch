FAQ on the Fractional CHRO Model

Is a Fractional CHRO only suitable for large companies?

No. SMEs, scale-ups, and growth-stage companies in particular benefit from this flexible C-level model.

How does this differ from interim management?

Interim roles are often transitional. Fractional engagements are longer-term, more strategic, and more deeply integrated into executive leadership.

Does a Fractional CHRO relieve the CEO?

Yes. Strategic people leadership is represented at C-level, while the CEO is relieved in key HR-related decision areas.

What does it really cost if we do not have dedicated HR leadership - and is a Fractional CHRO worth it in comparison?

The most common assumption is: no CHRO = no costs. That is not true. Without HR leadership, hidden costs emerge across different budget lines and are therefore rarely visible as one total picture - until the damage has already occurred. The table below shows a typical 3-year comparison for a company with 50-250 employees.

Recruiting without strategy Hidden 30-90k CHF 10-20k CHF 8-15k CHF
Estimated total cost / 3 years 170-470k CHF 105-225k CHF 280-400k CHF

Conclusion: "Do nothing" is, in most cases, the most expensive option - not because one single invoice appears, but because the costs remain invisible. A Fractional CHRO offers the best entry point: full HR competence, flexibly scalable, without the fixed-cost risk of a full-time role. Moving to a full-time CHRO only pays off once size and complexity truly require it - in practice typically from around 250-300 employees, and even then many companies still do not have a dedicated CHRO.
